CH-2 => Micro-Organisms : Friend & Foe

types of micro-organisms

  1. Bacteria - bacillus anthracis, bacillus cereus
  2. Fungi - aspergillus, penicillium
  3. Protozoa - amoeba, paramecium
  4. Algae - chlamydomonas, spirogyra
  5. Viruses - COVID-19, HIV

Uses of Micro-organisms

  1. They are used in the production of Antibiotics and Vaccines.
  2. A bacterium, named Lactobacillus, helps in the setting of curd from milk.
  3. Micro-organisms like Rhizobium and Blue-Green Algae help in fixing atmospheric nitrogen to increase the fertility of the soil.
  4. They are used in the biological treatment of sewage and industrial effluents.
  5. Micro-organisms like Yeast are used our homes to make idli and dosa. In baking industry, it is used in making breads, cakes and pastries. It is also used in the production of alcohol and wine on a large scale.
  6. Micro-organisms, such as certain bacteria, help in cleaning the enviroment by decomposing organic wastes into harmless and usable substances.
  7. Some micro-organisms are taken as Probiotics, that are believed to provide health benefits when consumed.

Diseases caused by Micro-organisms.

The micro-organisms that cause diseases are called Pathogens. They enter our body through the air we breathe, the water we drink or the food we eat. They can also get transmitted by direct contact with an infected person or carried by an animal. Microbial diseases that can spread from an infected person to a healthy person through air, water, food or physical contact are called communicable diseases. Examples of such diseases include Cholera, Common Cold, Chicken Pox and Tuberculosis.

When a person suffering from Common Cold sneezes, fine droplets of moisture carrying thousands of viruses are spread into the air. The virus may enter the body of a healthy person while breathing and cause infection.

Nitrogen cycle

Nitrogen Cycle is a biogeochemical process through which Nitrogen is converted into many forms, consecutively passing from the atmosphere to the soil to organism and back into the atmosphere. It involves several processes such as Nitrogen fixation, Nitrification, Denitrification, Decay and Putrefaction.

It is already mentioned above that the bacterium Rhizobium and Blue-Green Algae help in changing atmospheric nitrogen to nitrogenous compounds to enrich the fertility of the soil.
